# Publications and Applications

The USASSA dataset empowers researchers to conduct sophisticated analyses of how security assistance is employed, its utility, and its limitations[cite: 1]. 

## Foundational Literature

**Building Partner Capacity: US Aid to Security Sector Actors**  
*Patricia Sullivan, Giovanny Rincon Alvarez, and Nathan Marx*  
**Journal of Conflict Resolution (2024)**[cite: 1]  

**Abstract:** This article introduces the USASSA dataset. Our data clearly show not only the magnitude and geographic reach of US security assistance, but also its diversity[cite: 1]. While some security assistance is akin to humanitarian aid, other types of assistance blur the line between foreign aid and proxy warfare[cite: 1]. We demonstrate the utility of the dataset with an exploration of whether the effects of US security assistance on human rights violations and domestic terrorism vary across types of aid[cite: 1].
